Probabilistic Performance Analysis Using Phase-Type Distributions

Hakim Ziani, Advisors: Prof. A, Prof. B
European Performance Engineering Workshop (EPEW) 2024
Abstract: This paper presents a novel approach to probabilistic performance analysis using Phase-Type distributions. We develop methods for analyzing system performance metrics, particularly those exhibiting heavy-tailed behavior. Our framework enables accurate characterization of tail probabilities and extreme events, crucial for understanding system reliability and performance under adverse conditions. We demonstrate applications to computer systems, networks, and service infrastructures.
Keywords: EPEW Performance Analysis Phase-Type Distributions Probabilistic Modeling Tail Analysis Systems Engineering

Introduction

Performance analysis is crucial for understanding and optimizing complex systems. Traditional approaches often rely on exponential or Gaussian assumptions that fail to capture important characteristics of real-world performance data, particularly extreme events and tail behavior.

Phase-Type distributions offer a mathematically elegant solution, combining the analytical tractability of Markovian models with the flexibility to represent complex distributions. This work explores their application to performance engineering challenges.
